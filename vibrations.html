Physics of Vibrations Intro

Sound travels in sine and cosine waves. Their vibrations cause variations in air pressure, which in turn allows us to hear their sound by vibrating our ear drums. A crude formula for the vibrations caused in our ear by sound over time is e^-5t * sin(2pi*f*t), where t represents the time passed in seconds and f represents the frequency of the vibration. The frequency is the main determinant of what will make different noises sound different. For some context, if two notes are an octave apart, then the higher note’s frequency will be twice the other’s.
